Commercial EM Solver Comparison

Technical Blog · Solver Selection Guide
Choosing a commercial Electromagnetic (EM) solver is a trade-off between electrical size, geometric complexity, and frequency requirements. While high-frequency tools like Ansys HFSS and CST Studio Suite are industry standards, specialized tools like Remcom XFdtd and Keysight ADS Momentum dominate specific niches such as biomedical SAR or planar PCB design.

1. Finite Element Method (FEM)

The Gold Standard for Precision

The Finite Element Method (FEM) is a volumetric, frequency-domain approach that excels in modeling intricate 3D structures where small geometric details significantly impact performance.

Pro: Highly accurate for narrow-band, high-Q components like cavity filters or medical implants where dielectric inhomogeneity is critical.

Con: Because it requires meshing the entire computational volume — including the air — memory consumption grows rapidly as the object size (in wavelengths) increases.

2. Finite Difference Time Domain (FDTD)

The Broadband Workhorse

FDTD (and its close relative, FIT) solves Maxwell's equations by "marching" a pulse through a grid over time.

Pro: A single simulation provides broadband frequency data (S-parameters over a wide range). It is inherently parallelizable, allowing tools like Remcom XFdtd to utilize powerful GPU acceleration for massive speed gains.

Con: Not ideal for structures with long-lasting resonances (high-Q), as the simulation must run until the energy completely dissipates.

3. Method of Moments (MoM)

Optimized for Planar Designs

MoM is a boundary-element method that calculates currents on the surface of conductors rather than fields in a volume.

Pro: It avoids the "air mesh" entirely, making it incredibly fast for antennas and multi-layer PCBs (2.5D problems). Tools like Keysight ADS Momentum are the industry leaders for high-speed digital and RFIC layout verification.

Con: As complexity increases, the matrix inversion required for MoM becomes a bottleneck, often necessitating specialized solvers for large-scale problems.

4. Asymptotic Solvers (SBR+ / PO)

The Solution for Extreme Scale

When a problem involves objects thousands of wavelengths in size — such as a 5G base station in an urban city — full-wave solvers become impractical.

Pro: Shooting and Bouncing Rays (SBR+) treats EM waves like light rays, allowing for rapid calculation of coverage and interference in massive scenes.

Con: Accuracy is compromised for structures where wave-like effects (diffraction, complex coupling) are the dominant factors.
